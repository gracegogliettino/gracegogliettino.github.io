abstract: >-
  Quasars (QSOs), among the most luminous objects in the universe, hold promise as standard
  candles if their intrinsic luminosities can be accurately determined. Our study focuses on
  QSOs in the redshift range of 1.5─ 1.75, where rest-frame ultraviolet emission is shifted
  into the optical, observable in deep photometric surveys. Using multi-band ground-based data
  from the COSMOS 2020 Catalogue, we construct color-magnitude and color-color diagrams to
  investigate relationships between QSO properties and luminosity. Additionally, we have improved
  our sample selection by using the COSMOS Team's spectral template fits to better distinguish QSOs
  from star-forming galaxies with strong UV emission that have spectral energy distributions similar
  to QSOs. High-reliability QSOs are placed on a color-magnitude diagram to evaluate how effectively
  their colors can be used to predict their luminosities.
